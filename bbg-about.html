My Board Game Story

Every Friday night, my wife Libni and I struggled to find something fun to do. One rainy evening, we stumbled upon this quirky little game shop named Tiksa. Shelves were crammed with colorful board games—some dusty, others practically begging to be played. The shopkeeper suggested a game called Catan, so we decided to give it a try.

That night, our small apartment turned into a battlefield of trades and strategy. We laughed so hard arguing over sheep and bricks, and before we knew it, we were hooked. Fridays quickly became our board game night. We found gems like Ticket to Ride and Azul, even hosting game nights with friends.

What started as a simple way to kill time turned into something special. Those games didn’t just bring fun—they brought us closer, reminding us how much joy there is in the little things we share together.
